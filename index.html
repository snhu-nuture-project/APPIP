"What if we're still waiting on my transcripts?"

Acceptance Evaluation Pending (AEP) Permission

AEP enables you to enroll at SNHU pending the arrival and evaluation of official transcripts or other supporting documents. The advantage of AEP is that it lets you start at SNHU while waiting for transcripts from previous schools.

We recommend you speak with your admission counselor before pursuing AEP.

SNHU's mission is to help you earn your degree as quickly and as affordably as possible. One of the best ways we can do this is by evaluating all your transcripts from previous colleges to maximize your incoming transfer credits, which may:

  • Reduce the number of courses you need to take to earn your degree
  • Save you time and money

If, however, you're having difficulty obtaining your official school transcripts, you may still apply for Acceptance Evaluation Pending (AEP) status. AEP enables you to enroll at SNHU pending the arrival and evaluation of official transcripts or other supporting documents. The advantage of AEP is that it lets you start at SNHU while waiting for transcripts from previous schools.

We'd love for you to join us at SNHU as soon as you’re ready, but enrolling with AEP does carry some risk. Many factors determine if each class from a previous school can be applied for transfer credit in your SNHU degree program. Upon evaluation of your transcripts, you may not be awarded all the transfer credits you were expecting.

You may then end up having to re-take courses at SNHU for which you were expecting transfer credits. The more transfer credits you’re expecting, the greater the risk of not having them all accepted, and the more likely it is that you'll have to re-take those courses. We care about your long-term success, and setting realistic expectations is key to helping you achieve this.

At the end of your second term, your AEP status will end, and you will be accepted with all transfer credits that have been approved at that time.
